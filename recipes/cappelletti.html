Cappelletti: What is and Origin

The cappelletti is a stuffed pasta of peculiar shape, resembling a "little hat". The cappelletti are also filled inside with many options, such as beef, chicken, cheese and even nutmegs (although the latter is quite controversional).

The cappelletti has its origins in Romagna, a region in Italy, and its name comes from the term caplèt. This term is the name for stuffed pasta in Romagna dialect, with a reference to the pasta's shape as a galonza (a small hat with a brm and a large dome that the coutnry folk used to wear)

Ingredients

  • 4 3/4 cups type "00" or all purpose flour
  • 5 oz. Parmigiano Reggiano cheese, grated
  • 2 oz. mortadella
  • 2 oz. minced veal
  • 2 oz. minced beef
  • 2 oz. minced pork
  • 1/2 stick butter
  • 6 large eggs
  • High-quality meat broth
  • Nutmeg
  • Salt

Step-by-step

  1. Brown the minced meat in a pan with butter for a few minutes.
  2. Transfer it to a bowl and combine chopped mortadella, Parmigiano, a pinch of salt and nutmeg.
  3. Pour the flour onto a pastry board with a pinch of salt.
  4. Crack the eggs in the middle and knead them with a fork, then your hands.
  5. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  6. With a rolling pin, roll out the dough into a thin sheet.
  7. Cut out 1" squares using a ravioli cutter.
  8. Place a small walnut-sized portion of filling in the center of each round. Fold each one into a triangle and seal the edges, and bring the opposite ends together.
  9. Put the cappelletti on a cloth as you assemble them.
  10. Cook in a boiling broth.
  11. Finally, serve the cappelletti with a sprinkling of Parmigiano Reggiano.
  12. Enjoy your cappelletti!
